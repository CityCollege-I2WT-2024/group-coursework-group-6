To The Moon

On an autumn evening, during the volleyball championship, Jack , the star player of the Lunar Spikers, served a ball that soared into the night sky.

To everyone's shock, it hit the moon, leaving a fleeting mark before returning to Earth. The next day, headlines read, "Volleyball Player Accidentally Hits the Moon with the Ball."

Jack became famous, his serve a symbol of reaching for the stars. In celebration, the volleyball community threw a party so wild, even the moon blushed at the antics.

The Lunar Spikers, despite not winning the championship, were awarded a unique trophy crafted in the shape of the moon, commemorating the night when volleyball made history.

The trophy, which looked suspiciously like a giant cheese wheel, was presented by a NESA representative who quipped, "I guess you could say, Jacks's serve was truly out of this world - and now, we're all just a bunch of lunatics celebrating the cosmic blunder."

Jack , now a household name, often gazed upwards, wondering if somewhere up there, his ball was still bouncing, while the rest of us here on Earth were left with a story that would be volleyed back and forth for generations.
