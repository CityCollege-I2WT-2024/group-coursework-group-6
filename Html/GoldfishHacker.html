Goldfish Hacker Exposed: Tiny Tank Tech Gone Rogue

In what authorities are calling the "most unexpected cybercrime of the decade," a goldfish named Sparkles has been implicated in a high-tech hacking scheme targeting aquarium management systems across the globe.

The incident came to light after multiple luxury aquariums reported inexplicable changes to water temperatures, LED light patterns, and feeding schedules. Upon investigation, cybersecurity experts traced the attacks to a single tank in a local pet store, where Sparkles resided.

Equipped with a motion-sensing interface, the tank had been programmed to feed Sparkles whenever specific patterns of movement were detected. However, experts believe the crafty goldfish learned to exploit the system by repeatedly swimming in precise circles, unintentionally breaching the tank's control software and sending out rogue signals.
